What is ZolVault?

ZolVault is a yield vault — like a savings account that automatically finds the best interest rate, moves your money there, and reinvests your earnings, all without you doing anything after depositing.

You deposit zkLTC. An autonomous agent places it into yield pools, harvests the interest every hour, and reinvests it. Your LTC balance grows automatically through compounding.

ZolVault is the first autonomous yield vault on LitVM LiteForge testnet — launched April 2026. We deployed the first DeFi contracts on LiteForge.

LTC and LitVM

Litecoin (LTC) — launched 2011, 13 years of uninterrupted operation, faster and cheaper than Bitcoin. Until now, only used for payments. LitVM changes that.

LitVM — a Layer-2 built on top of Litecoin that adds smart contracts. Powered by Arbitrum Nitro, settles back to Litecoin L1 via zero-knowledge proofs. ZolVault's code runs on LitVM.

zkLTC — LTC bridged to LitVM. Same value as LTC, but on LitVM. Used to pay gas fees and deposit into ZolVault.

How yield works

Your deposited zkLTC goes into a ZolPool contract that generates yield. Yield = principal × APY × time elapsed. It's paid from a seeded reserve on testnet; on mainnet it comes from real trading fees.

The compounding snowball

Every hour, the agent calls compound() on-chain. It collects all accrued interest, takes 2% as a dev fee, and adds the remaining 98% back to your position. Next hour, you earn on a slightly bigger balance — that snowball is compounding.

Strategy rotation

Pool APYs change over time. The agent scans all pools and moves your money to the best one — but only if it's at least 3% better, to avoid unnecessary gas costs. All on-chain, real transactions.

Is it safe?

ZolVault is on testnet. The tokens are free test assets with zero real value. Get them from the faucet inside the Vault tab or at liteforge.hub.caldera.xyz.

What the contract guarantees

Principal never touched: Your deposit is stored as a fixed number. Fee math only runs on yield. Impossible to deduct from your deposit.

Emergency exit: Works even if the vault is paused. You can always get your funds out instantly.

Non-custodial: The contract holds funds, not us. Your keys, your money.

Professional audit planned before mainnet. Do not deposit real money — testnet only.

FAQ

First prompt: you're giving ZolVault permission to use your tokens (like giving someone a signed cheque). Second prompt: the actual deposit. Standard two-step safety in DeFi.
LTC is on the original Litecoin blockchain. zkLTC is LTC moved to the LitVM network — same value, different chain. ZolVault uses zkLTC because it runs on LitVM.
On testnet: tokens are worthless test assets. The contract guarantees principal returned 1:1. Smart contract bugs are always a theoretical risk in DeFi — that's why we audit before mainnet.
Every time compound() runs on-chain, the contract sends 2% of that cycle's gross yield to the developer wallet automatically. No manual claiming. More users = more compounds = more passive income.
